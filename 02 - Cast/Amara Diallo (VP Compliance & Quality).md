**Role:** VP Compliance & Quality · **Reports to:** [[Elena Vasquez (CEO)]] (via COO); dotted line to the Audit Committee

> [!quote] In character
> "A regulator can do in one letter what an attacker needs a whole kill-chain for. No outage, no breach — just a suspended accreditation, and thirty percent of our revenue stops taking new orders on Monday."

## Mandate
Owns **FCC and ITU filings, export control (ITAR/EAR), certification, audit** — and the **government security accreditation that gates the Defense segment** ([[Canon & Figures Register|30% of revenue]]). She holds the licenses without which there is neither service nor Defense revenue, and she keeps the accreditation enclave audit-clean for [[Tom Reuter (CISO)|Tom]].

## Objectives owned
- [[Canon & Figures Register|TPO-03]] — secure FCC full operational license by Q3 2026.
- [[Canon & Figures Register|TCO-02]] — maintain operating licenses & a flawless safety record (note: a debris event is also a *regulatory* event).

## What keeps them up at night
An **accreditation suspension** triggered by an incident or audit finding — *"the consequence is contractual from day one; there's no attribution delay to hide behind."* ITAR blocking the partner [[Henrik Sorensen (Program Director)|AURORA]] needs. A quiet FCC condition breach that becomes an investigation.
