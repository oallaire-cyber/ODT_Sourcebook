# 📐 SPICE Method Brief (domain-agnostic)
**SPICE — Scenario Planning to Identify Consequences Exposure.** Put a defensible figure on a catastrophic risk by quantifying its **consequences**, not guessing its **frequency**. Speaks EBIT and cash.

1. **Frame the business** — a perimeter with a P&L and a controlling function.
2. **Identify the scenario** — start from the most-feared event & the attacker's *business* objective; one plausible path proves it's real. Stop there.
3. **Walk the consequence timeline** — crisis hour → financial recovery. *Service recovery ≠ financial recovery.*
4. **Quantify with Finance** — three cases (best/realistic/pessimistic) of EBIT/FCF over the recovery horizon.
5. **Derive mitigations & re-quantify** — avoided loss = return on spend.
6. **Govern & reuse** — feed the graph; reuse context to make the next exercise cheaper.

**Triple validation** (why a board trusts it): Business recognises · Security confirms plausible · Finance prices it. → these are the `validated` stages on a [[_Scenario Stat-Block Template|SpiceScenario]].

**Domain-agnostic:** the worked example is cyber ([[BST-S1 NOC Ground Compromise (Cyber)|S1]]), but the same flow priced [[BST-GP1 Export-Control & Launch Disruption (Geopolitical)|geopolitical]], [[BST-RG1 Accreditation Suspension (Regulatory)|regulatory]] and [[BST-FN1 IPO Window Collapse (Financial)|financial]] scenarios — see [[Bestiary Index]].
